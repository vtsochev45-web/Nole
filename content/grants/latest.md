# 💰 Step-by-Step Guide to UK Farm Grants 2026

## Understanding the Post-Brexit Grant Landscape

The old Basic Payment Scheme (BPS) is ending. New Environmental Land Management (ELM) schemes are here. This guide explains what's available and how to apply.

## Current Grant Schemes

### 1. Sustainable Farming Incentive (SFI) 2026

**What it is:** Payments for environmentally friendly farming practices

**Payment rates (examples):**
| Action | Payment per hectare |
|--------|---------------------|
| Improved grassland | £151/ha |
| Moorland | £63/ha |
| Hedgerow management | £5/100m |
| Pollinator mix | £615/ha |

**Eligibility:**
- Minimum 3 hectares agricultural land
- Must be registered with RPA
- One SFI agreement per business
- Maximum £100,000/year

**Application windows:**
- June 2026: Small farms/those without existing ELM
- September 2026: All farms

**How to apply:**
1. Log into Rural Payments service
2. Check your land is registered
3. Choose actions that suit your farm
4. Submit application
5. Receive agreement within 3 months

### 2. Farming Investment Fund (FIF)

**What it is:** Grants for equipment and technology

**Themes:**
- **FETF (Equipment & Tech)** - £1,000-£25,000
- **Slurry Infrastructure** - Up to £250,000
- **Water Management** - Up to £500,000
- **Adding Value** - Up to £500,000

**2026 Deadlines:**
| Round | Opens | Closes |
|-------|-------|--------|
| FETF 2026 | 17 March | 27 April |
| Water Management | TBC | TBC |

**Application process:**
1. Expression of interest (online)
2. Full application (if shortlisted)
3. Approval and claim process
4. Installation and verification

### 3. Countryside Stewardship (CS)

**Higher Tier:**
- For complex environmental land management
- 5-20 year agreements
- Requires pre-application advice
- 142 capital items available

**Mid Tier:**
- Simpler options
- 5-year agreements
- Apply online

**Capital Grants:**
- Standalone grants (don't need full CS)
- Fencing, tree planting, water provision
- New round opens July 2026

### 4. Brexit Support - Delinked Payments

**Replacing BPS:**
- No application needed
- Automatic payments based on historical claims
- Phasing out: 98% reduction on first £30k by 2027

## Step-by-Step Application Guide

### Before You Apply

1. **Check eligibility:**
   - [ ] Registered with RPA
   - [ ] Land registered on Rural Land Register
   - [ ] No outstanding cross compliance issues
   - [ ] Meet scheme-specific requirements

2. **Gather documents:**
   - [ ] SBI (Single Business Identifier)
   - [ ] Field numbers and maps
   - [ ] Business bank statements
   - [ ] Previous grant agreements (if applicable)

3. **Plan your application:**
   - Which schemes suit your farm?
   - What actions can you realistically deliver?
   - Calculate income vs. effort required

### During Application

1. **Use correct portals:**
   - SFI: Rural Payments service
   - FIF: DEFRA online portal
   - CS: Natural England application

2. **Be accurate:**
   - Double-check field numbers
   - Verify land areas
   - Include all required evidence

3. **Save regularly:**
   - Applications can take hours
   - Save progress every 15 minutes
   - Screenshot confirmation page

### After Submission

1. **Record keeping:**
   - Save application reference number
   - Take screenshots of submission
   - Keep copies of all documents

2. **Response timeline:**
   - SFI: 3 months
   - FIF: 3-6 months
   - CS Higher: 6+ months

3. **If successful:**
   - Read agreement carefully
   - Note reporting requirements
   - Set calendar reminders for claims

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Applying for unsuitable actions**
- Only choose what you can realistically deliver
- Consider your farm type and resources

❌ **Incorrect field numbers**
- Always verify with RLR maps
- Mismatches cause delays/rejection

❌ **Missing deadlines**
- Set multiple reminders
- Submit 1 week before deadline

❌ **Poor record keeping**
- SFI requires 5 years of records
- Photograph everything
- Keep all receipts

❌ **Not understanding agreements**
- Read terms and conditions
- Know your obligations
- Understand penalty clauses
