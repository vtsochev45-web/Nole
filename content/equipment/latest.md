# 🚁 Farm Drones: How to Choose, Where to Fly, What They Cost

## Why Use Drones on Your Farm?

Drones offer UK farmers:
- **Crop monitoring** – spot disease, pests, irrigation issues early
- **Livestock tracking** – find animals, monitor herd health
- **Field mapping** – create accurate boundary maps, measure areas
- **Spraying** – targeted pesticide/fertilizer application
- **Insurance documentation** – aerial photos for claims

## Legal Requirements (CAA Regulations)

Before flying, you need:
1. **Operator ID** (£10/year) – register as drone operator
2. **Flyer ID** (free, online test) – prove you know rules
3. **Insurance** – public liability minimum £1m
4. **No-fly zones** – avoid airports, military bases, crowded areas

**Key rules:**
- Max height: 120m (400ft)
- Keep visual line of sight
- Don't fly over uninvolved people
- Stay 150m from built-up areas

## Total Cost Breakdown

**Entry Level:**
- Drone: £800-1,500
- Software: £50/month
- Training: £200
- Insurance: £150/year
- **Total Year 1: £2,400**

**Professional:**
- Drone: £15,000-35,000
- Software: £200/month
- Training: £500
- Insurance: £500/year
- **Total Year 1: £18,900**

## ROI Calculator

**Time saved per year:**
- Crop scouting: 40 hours → 5 hours (saves 35h)
- Boundary mapping: 16 hours → 2 hours (saves 14h)
- Livestock checks: 20 hours → 8 hours (saves 12h)

## 90-Day Startup Plan

**Month 1:**
- Register with CAA (Operator/Flyer ID)
- Buy insurance
- Purchase entry-level drone

**Month 2:**
- Practice flying in open fields
- Learn software basics
- First crop scouting missions

**Month 3:**
- Integrate into regular workflow
- Document improvements
- Consider upgrading if valuable
