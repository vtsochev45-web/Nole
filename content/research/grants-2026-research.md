# UK Farm Grants and Subsidies Research Report 2026

## Executive Summary

This report provides an overview of UK farm grants and subsidies available in 2026, focusing on updates to the Environmental Land Management (ELM) schemes, the Farming Investment Fund (FIF), Sustainable Farming Incentive (SFI) payments, and Brexit-related support schemes. Key findings include new application windows for SFI 2026, opening of the Farming Equipment and Technology Fund (FETF) 2026, ongoing delinked payments replacing the Basic Payment Scheme, and a controlled rollout of Countryside Stewardship Higher Tier (CSHT). Details on eligibility criteria, deadlines, and payment rates are provided.

---

## 1. Environment Land Management (ELM) Scheme Updates for 2026

### 1.1 Sustainable Farming Incentive (SFI) 2026

**Overview:** SFI pays farmers and land managers to adopt sustainable farming practices that benefit the environment, support food production, and improve productivity.

**Key Updates for 2026:**
- **Simplified Offer:** SFI26 reduces the number of actions from 102 (SFI24) to 71, removing 31 low‑uptake or low‑value actions.
- **Application Windows:**
  - **Window 1:** Opens **June 2026** for small farms (up to 50 ha) and farms without an existing ELM revenue agreement.
  - **Window 2:** Opens **September 2026** for all farms.
- **Eligibility:** Farmers must have at least **3 ha** of agricultural land.
- **Agreement Caps:**
  - Maximum agreement value: **£100,000 per year**.
  - Each farm business may hold only **one SFI26 agreement**.
  - Rotational actions cannot exceed the area declared in Year 1.
  - **Enhanced overwinter stubble (AHW7)** added to the existing 25% area limit cap (10 actions cannot exceed 25% of total agricultural area).
- **Payment Rate Changes:**
  - **Increased rates** for moorland livestock grazing and shepherding actions (UPL1, UPL2, UPL3, UPL8, UPL10).
  - **Reduced rates** for CSAM3 (Herbal leys), CAHL2 (Winter bird food), CNUM3 (Legume fallow) – applies only to new SFI26 agreements.
  - **Management payment (£20/ha)** discontinued to free up funding for more agreements.
- **Duration:** 5‑year actions become 3‑year actions to accommodate short‑term tenants.
- **Base & Supplemental Actions:** Supplemental actions can only be applied together with a base action in the same agreement.

**Sources:**
- Defra Farming Blog: [SFI26: details, definitions and what to expect](https://defrafarming.blog.gov.uk/2026/02/24/sfi26-details-definitions-and-what-to-expect/) (24 Feb 2026)
- GOV.UK: [Funding for farmers, growers and land managers](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/funding-for-farmers) (updated 24 Feb 2026)

### 1.2 Countryside Stewardship Higher Tier (CSHT)

**Overview:** CSHT pays for land management that protects, restores, or enhances the environment and mitigates climate change. It covers woodland, farmed land, and nature‑focused land.

**2026 Status:**
- **Opened:** September 2025 (rolling monthly invitations).
- **Application Process:** By invitation only after receiving pre‑application advice from Natural England or the Forestry Commission.
- **Agreement Length:** 5, 10, 15, or 20 years depending on the longest‑duration action.
- **Capital Items:** 142 capital items available; must be completed within 3 years of agreement start.
- **Eligibility:** Farmers, foresters, land managers with management control of the land. Public bodies may apply under certain conditions.
- **Payment:** Quarterly instalments; first payment in the fourth month after agreement start.

**Sources:**
- GOV.UK: [Countryside Stewardship Higher Tier (CSHT) guidance](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/countryside-stewardship-higher-tier-csht) (updated 19 Jan 2026)
- Applicant’s Guide: [CSHT applicant's guide](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/countryside-stewardship-higher-tier-csht/csht-applicants-guide)

### 1.3 Landscape Recovery

**Status:** Closed for new applications (last round closed earlier). Focuses on long‑term, large‑scale projects (≥500 ha) for net zero, protected sites, and wildlife‑rich habitat.

### 1.4 Capital Grants (ELM)

**Opening:** **July 2026** (guidance to be published ahead of applications).
**Purpose:** Funding for capital items to support environmental management actions.
**Existing capital grants** are available now.

**Source:** GOV.UK Funding page (updated 24 Feb 2026).

## 2. Farming Investment Fund (FIF) Current Status and Deadlines

### 2.1 Farming Equipment and Technology Fund (FETF) 2026

**Opening:** **17 March 2026**
**Closing:** **27 April 2026** (midday)
**Grant Value:** £1,000 – £25,000 per grant theme (Productivity, Slurry, Animal Health & Welfare).
**Total Budget:** £50 million (£20 million productivity, £20 million animal health & welfare, £10 million slurry management).
**Eligibility:** Businesses based and registered in England; farmers, horticulturalists, forestry owners, contractors.
**Animal Health & Welfare grant** only available for beef/dairy cattle, sheep, pigs, laying chickens, broiler chickens.
**Application:** Via the [Farming Investment Fund service](https://rpagrants.defra.gov.uk/welcome/).

## 3. Sustainable Farming Incentive (SFI) Payments

### 3.1 Payment Rates for SFI26 Actions

A detailed list of the 71 actions and their annual payments is available in the Defra blog post. Notable changes:

- **Moorland livestock grazing** (UPL1, UPL2, UPL3) and **shepherding** (UPL8, UPL10) rates increased.
- **Herbal leys (CSAM3)** reduced from £382/ha to **£224/ha**.
- **Winter bird food (CAHL2)** reduced from £853/ha to **£648/ha**.
- **Legume fallow (CNUM3)** reduced from £593/ha to **£532/ha**.

Payment rates are based on ‘income foregone plus costs’. The SFI management payment (£20/ha) is discontinued.

### 3.2 Eligibility Criteria for SFI26

- Minimum **3 ha** of agricultural land.
- **Window 1:** Small farms (≤50 ha) or farms without an existing ELM revenue agreement (SFI, CSMT, CSHT, HLS).
- **Window 2:** All farms (opens September 2026).
- Cannot exceed £100,000/year per agreement.
- Only one SFI26 agreement per farm business.
- Rotational actions cannot increase beyond Year 1 area.

### 3.3 Application Process

- Apply online via Rural Payments service.
- Full scheme guidance will be published before June 2026.
- Applications assessed on a first‑come, first‑served basis; windows may close early if budget is allocated.

**Source:** Defra Farming Blog (24 Feb 2026).

## 4. Brexit-Related Support Schemes

### 4.1 Delinked Payments (Replacing Basic Payment Scheme)

**Overview:** Delinked payments replace BPS from 2024–2027. No application required; payments made automatically to eligible businesses.

**Eligibility:** Must have claimed and been eligible for BPS in the 2023 scheme year (or inherited eligible land after 15 May 2023). Lump Sum Exit Scheme recipients are ineligible.

**Payment Calculation:** Based on average BPS payments from 2020–2022 (reference amount). Progressive reductions apply each year:

- **2026 & 2027 Reduction Bands:**
  - **£30,000 or less:** 98% reduction
  - **Amounts above £30,000:** 100% reduction

**Payment Schedule:** Single annual payments from **1 August** each year (2026 and 2027).

**Source:** GOV.UK [Delinked payments: replacing the Basic Payment Scheme](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/delinked-payments-replacing-the-basic-payment-scheme) (updated 10 Dec 2025).

### 4.2 Lump Sum Exit Scheme

**Status:** Closed (archived). Provided a lump sum for farmers leaving the industry.

### 4.3 Farming in Protected Landscapes (FiPL)

**Open:** Applications accepted year‑round.
**Deadline:** Projects must end by **March 2029**.
**Grant Value:** Varies by project.
**Purpose:** Support nature recovery, climate change mitigation, public access, and landscape quality in Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty (AONBs) and National Parks.

**Source:** GOV.UK Funding page (updated 24 Feb 2026).

## 5. Other Relevant Schemes

### 5.1 Animal Health and Welfare Review Funding

**Open:** Year‑round.
**Grant Value:** £215–£923 per review/follow‑up (cattle, sheep, pigs).
**Purpose:** Fund annual vet visits for health/welfare reviews and endemic disease follow‑ups.

### 5.2 England Woodland Creation Grant (EWCO)

**Open:** Year‑round.
**Grant Value:** Up to £10,200/ha plus up to £12,700/ha for wider benefits.
**Purpose:** Create new woodland (≥1 ha).

### 5.3 Woodland Management Plan Grant (PA3)

**Open:** Year‑round.
**Purpose:** Support 10‑year woodland management planning.

### 5.4 Woodland Creation Planning Grant (WCPG)

**Open:** Year‑round.
**Grant Value:** Max £30,500 per project.
**Purpose:** Prepare Woodland Creation Design Plan compliant with UK Forestry Standard.

### 5.5 Tree Health Pilot Scheme

**Open:** Year‑round.
**Grant Value:** Up to 80% of felling costs per m³, up to 40% of infrastructure costs.
**Purpose:** Slow spread of pests/diseases affecting ash, larch, spruce, sweet chestnut, oak.

### 5.6 Woodland Tree Health (WTH) Grant

**Open:** Year‑round.
**Purpose:** Restock or improve woodland after tree health problems.

## 8. Summary of Changes for 2026

- **SFI:** Simplified offer (71 actions), new application windows, increased moorland rates, reduced rates for three actions, removal of management payment, £100k cap per agreement.
- **FETF:** Opens 17 March 2026 with £50 million budget across three themes.
- **CSHT:** Invitation‑based rollout continues; pre‑application advice mandatory.
- **Delinked Payments:** Progressive reductions continue; 2026 payments reduced by 98% (first £30k) and 100% (above £30k).
- **Capital Grants:** New round opening July 2026 (details pending).

Farmers should monitor the Defra Farming Blog and GOV.UK “Funding for farmers” page for latest updates.
