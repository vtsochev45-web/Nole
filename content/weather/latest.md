# 🌡️ Preparing Your UK Farm for Heatwaves: Complete 2026 Guide

## Climate Reality: UK Heatwaves Are Increasing

2025 saw record temperatures across Britain. 2026 is forecast to bring more extreme heat events. This guide helps you prepare.

## Region-Specific Heat Risks

### 🔥 South-East England (Highest Risk)
- **Risk level:** Very High
- **Typical peaks:** 35-40°C
- **Vulnerable:** Horticulture, poultry, dairy
- **Key prep:** Irrigation, shade structures

### 🌡️ Midlands & East Anglia (High Risk)
- **Risk level:** High
- **Typical peaks:** 32-38°C
- **Vulnerable:** Arable, root crops
- **Key prep:** Soil moisture, early harvesting

### 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 Scotland & North (Moderate Risk)
- **Risk level:** Moderate
- **Typical peaks:** 28-32°C
- **Vulnerable:** Livestock (not acclimated)
- **Key prep:** Ventilation, water access

## Water Management Strategies

### Immediate Actions (Do Now)
1. **Check water storage** – Clean tanks, repair leaks
2. **Test pumps** – Ensure backup power
3. **Map water points** – Ensure livestock access
4. **Calculate needs:**
   - Dairy cows: 100-150L/day each in heat
   - Beef cattle: 40-80L/day
   - Sheep: 4-8L/day
   - Pigs: 15-30L/day

### Medium-Term (This Month)
- Install additional troughs
- Create water redundancy (multiple sources)
- Consider rainwater harvesting expansion
- Check abstraction licenses

### Long-Term (Before Summer)
- Reservoir expansion
- Drip irrigation for high-value crops
- Shade infrastructure

## Livestock Cooling Solutions

### Shade Structures
| Type | Cost | Effectiveness | Best For |
|------|------|---------------|----------|
| Natural tree belts | Low | High | Pastures |
| Permanent sheds | £5,000-20,000 | Very High | Dairy yards |
| Shade cloth | £500-2,000 | Medium | Temporary |
| Umbrellas | £200-500 | Low | Individual animals |

### Active Cooling
- **Misting systems:** £2,000-8,000 (very effective)
- **Fans:** £100-500 each (barns/poultry)
- **Wallowing areas:** £500-2,000 (pigs/cattle)

### Species-Specific Tips

**Dairy Cows:**
- Most vulnerable to heat stress
- Need shade + ventilation + water
- Consider night milking in heatwaves
- Watch for panting, drooling, lethargy

**Sheep:**
- Shear before peak summer
- Provide shade in afternoon
- Check flies (heat increases problems)

**Pigs:**
- High risk (can't sweat)
- Wallowing essential
- Ventilated housing critical
- Frozen treats help

**Poultry:**
- Highest mortality risk in heat
- Max ventilation
- Reduce stocking density
- Provide electrolytes in water

## Crop Protection

### Before Heatwave
- **Irrigate deeply** 2-3 days before (not shallow)
- **Avoid spraying** – droplets magnify heat
- **Check mulch** – maintain 5cm+ organic layer
- **Harvest early** if crops near ready

### During Heatwave
- **Morning irrigation only** (5-9 AM)
- **Shade cloth** for vegetables (£1-2/m²)
- **Mist systems** for high-value crops
- **Stop fertilizer** – increases water needs

### After Heatwave
- **Assess damage** – photograph for insurance
- **Water gradually** – don't flood shocked plants
- **Check for disease** – heat stress = vulnerability
- **Plan improvements** for next year

## Emergency Response Checklist

### 24 Hours Before Forecast Heat
- [ ] All water troughs full and flowing
- [ ] Shade structures deployed
- [ ] Emergency contacts list updated
- [ ] Vet on standby notice
- [ ] Check insurance coverage

### During Heatwave
- [ ] Check animals every 2 hours
- [ ] Monitor water consumption
- [ ] Activate cooling systems
- [ ] Document temperatures
- [ ] Postpone non-essential work

### Post-Heatwave
- [ ] Full health check all livestock
- [ ] Inspect crops for damage
- [ ] Review what worked/didn't
- [ ] Update plans for next event
- [ ] Claim insurance if applicable
